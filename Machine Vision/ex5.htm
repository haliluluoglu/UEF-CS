Exercise 5: Spatial filtering

In these exercises, two source images are used: an industrial circuit board corrupted by salt-and-pepper noise, and a slightly blurred image of the North Pole of the Moon.

    1. Median filter the circuit board image (medfilt2). Experiment with different filter sizes. What happens when the median filter size increases? Check also how different image padding methods (zero-padding and symmetric padding) affect on filtering.

    2. Matlab's help for colfilt shows an example of mean filtering (web help). Mean filter the circuit board image; try also max and min filters. For what purposes might these filters be useful?

  2. Matlab function edge can be used for edge detection. Find the edges from the circuit board image; use both Sobel and Laplacian of Gaussian methods. Repeat the exercise for median and mean filtered images. In your opinion, which combination produces the best result?

  3. The general image filtering command in Matlab is imfilter, and predefined filters can be created with fspecial. Sharpen the Moon image by using a suitable filter. Does sharpening enhance the circuit board image?
